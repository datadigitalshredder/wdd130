Specified Crops

Brassicas

×

These include vegetables such as cabbage, cauliflower, brocolli, and mustard. Carry out a nutrient analysis prior to planting. Brassicas typically require high phosphorus and potassium and lower nitrogen blends at planting (Compound C, Vegetable blend or equivalent). High basal rates can be required, however, avoid placing the fertilizer too close to the seedling to avoid fertilizer toxicity. Nitrogen applications can be made as a topdressing throughout the season.

Common Pest: Diamondblack moth

Cotton

×

Carry out a nutrient analysis prior to planting. Cotton typically requires high phosphorus and potassium and lower nitrogen blends at planting (Cotton blend 6:15:12 +5S +0.1B or Compound L 5:17:10 +6S + 0.1B). Avoid placing the fertilizer too close to the seedling to avoid fertilizer toxicity. Calcium Ammonium Nitrate (CAN) can be topdressed throughout the vegetative growth stages of the crop.

Maize

×

Carry out a nutrient analysis prior to planting. Maize typically requires a high phosphorus blend and moderate amounts of potassium/nitrogen at planting (Comp. D, Double D or Cereal blend). Nitrogen applications can be made as a topdressing up until tasseling. Topdressing applications of potassium can in some instances improve grain development and assist with reducing drought stress.

Common pest: Fall armyworm.

Onions

×

Carry out a nutrient analysis prior to planting. Onions typically require high phosphorus and potassium and lower nitrogen blends at planting (Compound C, Vegetable blend or equivalent). High basal rates can be required and often broadcasted and mixed into the soil. It is best to apply to the soil at least one week prior to planting/transplanting) to avoid fertilizer toxicity. Calcium Nitrate applications can be made as a topdressing throughout the season. Apply routinely at low rates.

Common Pest: Thrips

Peas

×

Carry out a nutrient analysis prior to planting. Peas are best suited to well drained fertile soils (sandy loam). Peas typically requires a blend with high potassium, phosphorus and only small amounts of nitrogen at planting ("Compound C"). Potassium, Nitrogen, and Calcium products should be topdressed through out the season.

Potatoes

×

Carry out a nutrient analysis prior to planting. Potatoes typically require high phosphorus and potassium and lower nitrogen blends at planting (Potatoe Blend 8:18:24 5S + 0.06B + 0.4 Zn or equivalent). Very high basal rates can be required, however, avoid placing the fertilizer too close to the seedling to avoid fertilizer toxicity. Potassium Nitrate, SOP or Calcium Nitrate can be topdressed throughout the vegetative growth stages of the crop.

Common Pest: Leaf miner

Soya Beans

×

Carry out a nutrient analysis prior to planting. Soyas are best suited to clayey loam soils (Red soils). Soya typically requires a high potassium blend, moderate amounts of phosphorus, and only small amounts of nitrogen at planting ("Soya blend"). Excessive use of Nitrogen is not recommended as it can negatively affect root nodulation which will be counterproductive to the crop.

Tobacco

×

Carry out a nutrient analysis prior to planting. Tobacco typically requires a high phosphorus and potassium and lower nitrogen (with low chlorine) blends at planting (Tobacco blend 10:24:20 + 6S (max 4% Cl) or 6:28:23 +5S +0.1B (max 4% Cl) equivalent). High basal rates can be required. Avoid placing the fertilizer too close to the seedling to avoid fertilizer toxicity. Potassium Nitrate, SOP or Calcium Nitrate, Ammonium Nitrate or Urea can be topdressed throughout the vegetative growth stages of the crop, however, in most cases, early applications are advised. The exact amount of nitrogen required for a tobacco crop is determined by soil texture, timing of ploughing, rainfall, residual nitrogen and crop variety type. Excessive nitrogen can lead to the crop maturing slowly and increasing the susceptibility to diseases.

Common pest: Eelworm

Tomatoes

×

Carry out a nutrient analysis prior to planting. Tomatoes typically require high phosphorus and potassium and lower nitrogen blends at planting (Compound C, Vegetable blend or equivalent). High basal rates can be required, however, avoid placing the fertilizer too close to the seedling to avoid fertilizer toxicity. Potassium Nitrate, SOP or Calcium Nitrate can be top-dressed throughout the vegetative growth stages of the crop.

Common pest: Tuta absoluta

Wheat/ Barley

×

Carry out a nutrient analysis prior to planting. Wheat typically requires a high phosphorus blend and moderate amounts of potassium/nitrogen at planting (Comp. D, Double D or Cereal blend). Nitrogen applications can be made as a topdressing up until flowering. Late nitrogen applications can increase the protein concentration of the grain which may be required according to the purchasing market.

Common pest: Quelea
